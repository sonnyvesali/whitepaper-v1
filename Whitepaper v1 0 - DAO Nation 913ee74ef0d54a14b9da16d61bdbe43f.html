Introduction

The Problem

The institutional Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) efforts for many years have been at the mercy of spending power and influenced by hostile actors. These forces have no interest in solving humanity's most pressing problems.

On the environmental side of ESG, impact projects have ranged from increasing biodiversity, coral reef de-acidification, water depletion, and many more efforts to ward off a climate crisis. The social side of ESG has seen both climate and social activism, with projects that range from protecting individual rights and gender equality to sustainable lifestyle change and veganism. Whether the social impact project is aimed at preserving the Amazon or protecting global gay rights, they are all failing to co-build a better future for the same reasons:

  1. They lack transparent capital allocation
  1. The lack of greener, geographically translatable solutions
  1. They do not achieve physical decarbonization at scale
  1. They are under-funding and underdeveloped in regenerative sectors.

Climate risk poses an existential problem to all businesses in the global economy. Regenerative businesses, beginning to be understood as “ReFi” or Regenerative Finance, are enabled by tokens and tokenomics, blockchain technology, decentralized ownership, and innovative strategies. Regenerative businesses will address transparent and efficient resource allocation based on merit, sustainability, and long-term social impact. The unlock of both the strategy and tools to accomplish this will be a massive force for regenerative businesses to mobilize and coordinate global communities. They will empower their members through frictionless, decentralized change. DAO Nation will sit at the forefront of this change and seeks to build a fully decentralized ecosystem, that provides a sustainable, collaborative and financial infrastructure as an ESG-focused incubator.

The Mission

DAO Nation will sit at the forefront of this change and seeks to build a fully decentralized ReFi ecosystem, that provides a sustainable, collaborative and financial infrastructure as an ESG-focused incubator.

We hold a vision of ReFi as the next stage of crypto and web3 that is funding, supporting, and connecting regenerative projects and teams. These projects have been traditionally underfunded and underserved, creating impact blindspots. We see a future that is solving the world’s most pressing problems and aligns incentives toward good and circular economies.

DAO Nation’s strategy for achieving this vision:

  • Build the technical infrastructure for global ReFi adoption.
  • With access to capital, knowledge, and talent we will be able to effectively support a wide array of ReFi initiatives and contribute to making the world a better place.
  • Leveraging the power of collaborative governance to establish a regenerative circular economy.
  • Affect all aspects of ESG for both individuals and groups through:
    1. Education
    1. Funding
    1. Community Engagement

DAO Nation's goal is to provide infrastructure & tooling for regenerative businesses on a global scale. so they can focus on solving the world’s most pressing problems. This includes funding capable founders, getting them connected to investors & peers, and educating and empowering future founders.

DAO Arms

There will be 5 key DAO Arms to kickoff DAO Nation, which will operate autonomously as subDAOs:

1. Product Development

The Product Development team exists to build products that empower the community to build the future of regenerative finance. This includes designing new features, building new products, and improving current products.

2. Governance & Coordination

The Governance & Coordination team is responsible for understanding the state & helping each of the subDAOs, and to act as the facilitator of collaboration between the subDAOs to accomplish DAO Nation’s mission. This includes implementing transparency models, creating synergies, and managing the hiring process for the various subDAOs.

3. Growth & Marketing

The Growth & Marketing teams are responsible for the growth of DAO Nation’s presence in the ReFi ecosystem, as well as for supporting its subDAOs. This takes many forms like social media campaigns, holding off or online events, hosting joint hackathons,

4. Community & Education

The Community & Education team is responsible for creating educational materials, and workshops, and facilitating community dialogue. Along with that, streamlining the process to help onboard new members into DAO Nation and into an appropriate subDAO, and getting them ready to handle tasks and responsibilities of being a DAO contributor.

5. Incubation & Investments

The Incubation & Investments team conducts research and due diligence on potential impact investments, as well as projects/teams that apply for funding through the incubator. They ensure the validity of the provided information, provide a thorough analysis of protocols and markets, and present their findings to the GP committee. The investment arm’s advisory team works alongside incubated projects to provide support and monitor progress. The I&I teams also deliver big-picture market insights to inform DAO Nation’s overall direction and strategy.

Tokenomics as Governance

DAO Nation is allocating 40% of the total supply of the $ESG token to the treasury to be ‘mined’ by the community through retainers, initiatives, or any activity that is in the long-term interest of the DAO. This process is formally called Governance Mining, where community members can earn $ESG rewards through direct participation in the network.

Participation implies generating growth and decentralization by impacting governance events, contributing to any of DAO Nations’ arms, or building a ventureDAO - an autonomous DAO within the DAO Nation ecosystem.

This ensures that the most active actors in the community get more influence over the future of the DAO.

Working Groups are the proposed format for community members to band together as teams within the different parentDAO arms, or incubated projects aimed to be spawned as a ventureDAO.

Each working group will submit its proposals and budget requests to its relevant DAO arm. Working Groups will submit updates on key KPIs on their progress to allow transparency and data for community members to vote on.

A differentiation that should be made between subDAOs:

  • DAO Arms, or Department subDAOs, are comprised of various working groups operating as teams within each arm.
  • Venture subDAOs are entrepreneurial working groups that spin out as their own sovereign DAOs.

DAO Nation plans to finance both of these - department working groups via governance mining, and ventureDAOs via direct funding made by the Investment Arm.

As far as subDAO-driven governance is concerned, each working group will be given a non-transferrable NFT as their working group vote on a proposal. For the obvious conflict of interest reasons, working group members cannot vote in parentDAO decisions directly related to them.

Token Utility

DAO Nation’s $ESG token will be utilized by holders throughout the DAOs’ ecosystem of products and services, from staking & bonding to participating in verified IDOs on the launchpads.

Members who stake their tokens accumulate veESG - the native governance token, so that they can take part in the parentDAOs’ voting and applying proposals. Governance participants will then be rewarded with $ESG for their contributions.

Token holders will also be able to participate in liquidity pools to provide and increase liquidity for $ESG with other tokens, and then use DAO Nation’s bonding feature.

veESG

As mentioned above, in order to participate in the governance process of DAO Nation and form various working groups - $ESG holders will be required to lock up their $ESG in staking contracts.

$ESG stakers will be rewarded with the non-transferrable governance token veESG.

Longer lock-up periods result in greater veESG rewards. As a result, the people with the most confidence get more influence on community governance matters. To further incentivize governance participation those who actively vote and submit proposals get $ESG in return.

Incentivizing staking and governance participation comes with the following benefits:

  • Rewards long-term holders
  • Drives community engagement
  • Decentralization of decision-making

DAO Nation Infrastructure Roadmap

To enable DAO Nation to apply its vision for global-scale impact, we will launch a diversified array of products and tools, to allow its members streamlined access to liquidity, funding, and knowledgebase.

All products and programs mentioned below will be implemented during Phase 1 of DAO Nation’s launch, in Q2 2022:

Token Launchpad

From IDOs to presales, token launchpads are one of the most practical ways for an emerging web3 project to collect funding by distributing a portion of its token supply as a de-facto pre/seed round and bootstrap its liquidity.

As its first infrastructure product, DAO Nation has built a custom, permissionless launchpad that will enable venture subDAOs and other ecosystem projects to raise community funding, a decentralized alternative VC backing.

A Verified Presale or IDO is a project that was either spawned as a ventureDAO or joined the DAO Nation ecosystem.

Any verified presale/IDO is promoted within and by the DAO Nation ecosystem, which also provides initial funding/liquidity to assist with pool bootstrapping, as well as applying a network effect and synergies between early-stage protocols.

Gamified NFT minting capabilities will be added to allow the creation of ReFi-centric pfp communities.

Bonds

Bonding is a mechanism in which a user can sell assets to a protocol in exchange for DAO Nation’s native token. By implementing bonds, users can purchase $ESG tokens at a discounted rate directly from its platform.

Bond prices are determined by the supply and demand of the bonds and include a vesting period to prevent arbitrage exit.

By offering $ESG bonds, DAO Nation’s treasury will be able to diversify with high-yielding assets, stablecoins, and protocol-owned liquidity.

ReFinder

With the emergence of ReFi, we identified a lack of viable tools to compare and analyze new projects in the field, a clear need from both contributors and investors.

The most established general crypto analytics firms act as generalists in their analysis, and as a result, no real depth or insight can be derived from said analysis. ReFinder fixes this critical issue by focusing the analysis strictly on ReFi DAOs with a focus on quality and depth of analysis over quantity. What this means is dedicated metrics for the measurable KPIs of a DAO, like treasury size, number of unique protocol users, and expert analysis of protocol fundamentals and value propositions.

ReFi Index & ETF

The number of ReFi protocols is growing at a rapid pace.

DAO Nation will be introducing a ReFi index to accurately track the market sentiment, utilizing on-chain and off-chain data on financials and alternative data points to create proprietary ratings.

In addition, we are building ETF products that provide institutional and private investors with diversified exposure to the ReFi market.

DAO Nation Fellowships

The main problem with building a DAO Aggregator, whose goal is to connect new contributors and founders together, is that the founders of new companies have even less tolerance for inexperienced labor than an established company. This is due to the fact that a founder can only plan as far as the runway allows. In practice, Coinbase can afford to take a much longer-term approach to hire talent than a new DAO. It’s much harder to only have one side of the equation like entry-level positions or ambitious inexperienced labor on their own. This analysis of the talent shortage specifically in ReFi has led us to the conclusion that the fact that all solutions must have the pipeline of ambitious inexperienced labor to entry-level opportunities upfront.

DAO Nation plans to be a nexus point for onboarding - not only for ReFi, but the entire ecosystem. The path to ensure this with the highest probability is to perform the whole training process in-house - from the basics to opportunities to beyond.

This guarantees for all DAO Nation fellows that on the other side of each mentorship is a stack of opportunities & bounties, issued by DAO Nation, to hone their skills. These can be anywhere from protocol analysis bounties, bug fixes, new projects, and everywhere in between, that are exclusive to new DAO members.

As they graduating the program - members can join/found working groups, start completing public bounties, and participate in the governance mining to collect $ESG rewards.

DAO Incubation as a Service

Bottom-up innovation in the DAO Nation ecosystem’s main goal.

We aim for rapid expansion of the ReFi ecosystem through venture subDAO investments. The most battle-tested approach to growing any entrepreneurship-driven system is to give ambitious people capital, advice, resources, and practice the non-interference policy except in extreme cases. A DAO incubator where we share with protocol founders our ecosystem network - product development, recruiting, partnerships, and fundraising to support their vision.

The parentDAO will determine budgets for the Incubation-as-a-Service (IAAS) subDAO on an ongoing basis. This allows us to ramp up activity gradually as the treasury grows.

Projects are subject to thorough qualitative analysis, and the findings will ultimately be presented to the investment arm’s GP committee for review and potential on-chain approval.

Project Research & Incubation Proposal Process

The Project Research Proposal group submits an application on behalf of an eligible project they’ve identified with the intent of writing a full due-diligence report. If approved, the research working group will be compensated with $ESG from governance mining. These findings will be attached to a Project Incubation Proposal which is subject to review by the GP committee who will decide whether or not to invest.

Through this process, DAO Nation aims to invest primarily in talented community members, some will originate from its Fellowship programs. During the incubation, the team will remain a working group. When a product or prototype is finalized, they’ll form a ventureDAO.

For follow-on funding requests and direct investing in outside projects, DAO Nation’s research and due diligence working groups will perform qualitative research and due diligence. Its analysis template will include, but not be limited to the following:

  1. Eligibility
    1. Does the protocol align with DAO Nation’s mission?
    1. Are the proposed costs compatible with the deal sizes defined in the budget?
  1. Product
    1. Does the product introduce any novelties?
    1. Do they bring meaningful improvements vs existing protocols?
    1. What is their revenue model?
    1. Have they established a product-market fit?
    1. What partnerships and integrations are they pursuing?
      1. Have they secured any as of yet?
  1. Project management
    1. Are the milestones for the project clearly defined?
    1. Is there a well-defined plan for the longer term?
    1. Do they have a clear breakdown of use of funds?
      1. Are the costs based on quotes or approximations?
  1. Tokenomics
    1. How is or will the token be distributed at genesis?
    1. To what extent do token holders have a say in governance?
    1. Does the issuance model help facilitate improvements within the protocol?
    1. Is there a model for capturing and distributing value to token holders?
    1. What are the plans for securing and maintaining liquidity?
    1. Are there/will there be productive use cases for the token?
  1. Market
    1. What is the market size?
      1. What is the addressable market size?
    1. What are the projections for the total and addressable markets going forward?
    1. What does the competitive landscape look like?
      1. Who are the direct competitors?
      1. Who are the indirect competitors?
  1. Team
    1. Does the team have relevant experience?
      1. Do they have an online track record?
    1. Are they active within the community?
    1. To what degree do they know the market they are about to enter?
      1. What level of research have they done prior to seeking funding for their project?
  1. Governance
    1. Are there admin keys?
      1. If multisig has the final say, what powers does the multisig hold?
        1. How long between the vote and final sign-off?
        1. How many signers to form a consensus?
    1. Are contributors within the DAO active participants in governance?
      1. Is there a healthy level of debate around proposals?
    1. What does the governance infrastructure look like?
    1. What is the quality of the governance process?

Depending on the level of resources needed by the incubated protocol, DAO Nation will keep 5-15% of the token supply in return. Based on the research, the Investment Arm’s elected GP committee makes the final decision on whether to invest or not.
